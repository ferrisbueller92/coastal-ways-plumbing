The Big Idea

V1 recommended off-the-shelf software. V2 throws that out and builds exactly what Beau needs instead. Here's why.

V1 Plan (SaaS)

$72+

per month

ServiceM8 ($29) + Xero ($42) + extras = $72+/month in subscriptions, every month, forever. Plus learning 3-4 different apps with features Beau will never touch.

V2 Plan (Custom)

$0

per month

Custom tools built on free infrastructure. One app that does exactly what Beau needs. Emma uses Google Sheets she already knows. No subscriptions. No bloat.

Why build instead of buy?

Not because we're cheap. Because custom tools have zero friction.

  • ServiceM8 has 50+ features. Beau needs about 6 of them. Every extra feature is a button he'll never tap, a menu he has to scroll past, a setting that confuses things. Our custom app has exactly what he needs and nothing he doesn't.
  • No learning curve. The app is designed around how Beau already works. Create job, quote it, invoice it, get paid. That's it.
  • No monthly drain. $72/month is $864/year. Over 5 years, that's $4,320 gone. Our tools cost nothing to run.
  • Emma already knows spreadsheets. No training her on Xero. She opens Google Sheets, sees the data, does what she does.
  • We can build features that don't exist. Like an AI that learns Beau's pricing history and suggests quotes. No SaaS tool offers that because they don't have Beau's data.

Exception: Xero stays as an option

Accounting software is genuinely hard to replicate, and Beau's accountant may require it for BAS lodgement. But for the first 6 months, Google Sheets IS the financial ledger. If and when an accountant needs Xero, we'll plug it in. Until then, we don't pay for it.

Section 2

Architecture Overview

How all the pieces connect. It's simpler than it looks — four layers, all free, all proven.

Beau's Phone

PWA — looks and works like a native app. Add to home screen once, done. Works offline in crawl spaces with no signal.

↓ syncs over internet ↑

Google Apps Script

Free middleman. A small program that handles requests between the phone app and the spreadsheet. Like a receptionist for data.

↓ reads & writes ↑

Google Sheets

THE database. One spreadsheet workbook with six tabs: Jobs, Customers, Quotes, Invoices, Expenses, Reviews. All data lives here.

↓ opens directly

Emma's Computer

Opens the same Google Sheet. Sees all data in the spreadsheet format she already knows. Filter, sort, export — all standard.

What's a PWA?

Progressive Web App. It's a website that behaves like a phone app. Beau opens a link, taps "Add to Home Screen," and it appears on his phone with its own icon. No App Store, no updates to install, no storage taken up. It just works.

Offline? Yes. When Beau's in a crawl space with no signal, the app still works. Data saves locally and syncs when he's back online.

What's Google Apps Script?

Think of it as a free robot that sits between Beau's phone and the spreadsheet. When Beau creates a job on his phone, the robot writes it to the spreadsheet. When Emma updates something in the spreadsheet, the phone sees it next time it syncs. All free, all Google.

What about hosting?

App: GitHub Pages (free, unlimited, used by millions of sites).
Data: Google Sheets (free, backed up, version history).
API: Google Apps Script (free, runs on Google's servers).
Emails: EmailJS (free tier, 200 emails/month).

Total hosting: $0/month

Is it reliable?

Google Sheets has 99.9% uptime (same infrastructure as Gmail and Google Search). GitHub Pages is used by Microsoft, Facebook, and millions of developers worldwide. EmailJS sends 500 million+ emails per year. These aren't experimental — they're boring, proven infrastructure.

Section 3

What We're Building

Eight tools, each solving a specific problem. Built in priority order so Beau gets value from week one.

Tool 1

Job Board

Validated Week 1

A mobile-first dashboard showing today's jobs, upcoming jobs, and completed jobs. Beau can create a new job in 30 seconds — customer name, address, service type, notes. Tap a phone number to call the customer. Tap an address to open Maps and navigate there.

Why build it: ServiceM8 charges $29/mo for this. Our version does ONE thing perfectly — track jobs — without 50 features Beau won't use. It's faster to open, faster to use, and costs nothing.

How Beau Uses It
  1. Open the app on his phone (home screen icon)
  2. See today's jobs at a glance — addresses, times, service types
  3. Tap "+" to create a new job
  4. Fill in: customer name, phone, address, service type, date/time, notes
  5. Save — job appears on the board instantly
  6. Tap a job to update status: Scheduled → In Progress → Complete
  7. Marking a job as "Complete" triggers a prompt: "Create invoice?"
  8. Tap the phone icon to call the customer, or the map icon to navigate
Data Created

Jobs tab — one row per job with all details, status, and timestamps. Customers tab — auto-created if the customer is new (no duplicate entry needed).

Complexity Medium
Priority Week 1
Running Cost $0/mo
Tool 2

Quote Generator

Validated Week 1

Generate a branded PDF quote on-site from Beau's phone. Select the job type, add line items (labour, materials, callout fee), the tool calculates GST and total automatically, generates a Coastal Ways-branded PDF, and emails it to the customer. All in under two minutes.

Why build it: Quoting from templates with auto-calculation saves 15+ minutes per quote vs handwriting or typing in a notes app. A professional branded PDF also sets Beau apart from competitors who text a number.

How Beau Uses It
  1. From a job, tap "Create Quote"
  2. Select the service type (e.g. blocked drain, hot water, general plumbing)
  3. Add line items — each with description, quantity, and unit price
  4. GST calculates automatically (10% of subtotal)
  5. Preview the branded PDF — CWP logo, colours, ABN, professional layout
  6. Tap "Send to Customer" — quote goes via email with the PDF attached
  7. Quote is saved to the Quotes tab with status "Sent"
  8. When customer accepts, tap "Accepted" — status updates, ready to schedule
Data Created

Quotes tab — quote ID, linked job, line items, subtotal, GST, total, status (Draft/Sent/Accepted/Declined), and dates.

Technical Note

Uses html2pdf.js for PDF generation — renders HTML to PDF directly in the browser. No server needed. The PDF is generated on Beau's phone and sent via EmailJS.

Complexity Medium
Priority Week 1
Running Cost $0/mo
Tool 3

Invoice Generator

Validated Week 1

Auto-generates a branded tax invoice from a completed job. Beau taps one button and the customer gets a professional invoice that meets all ATO requirements. He can also mark invoices as paid and track outstanding amounts at a glance.

Why build it: Invoicing should be ONE TAP from a completed job, not a separate process in a separate app. If a quote already exists for the job, the line items are pre-filled — zero re-entry.

How Beau Uses It
  1. Complete a job — app prompts "Create Invoice?"
  2. Line items pre-fill from the quote (or enter manually if no quote)
  3. Preview the branded tax invoice — all legal requirements included
  4. Tap "Send" — customer receives invoice via email
  5. When payment comes in, tap "Mark Paid"
  6. Select payment method: Cash, Bank Transfer, or Card
  7. Invoice status updates — visible to Emma in the spreadsheet immediately
Invoice Requirements (ATO Compliant)
  • Business name: Coastal Ways Plumbing
  • ABN: 62 284 909 142
  • "Tax Invoice" header (mandatory for GST-registered businesses)
  • Sequential invoice number (CWP-0001, CWP-0002...)
  • Invoice date and due date
  • Customer name and details
  • Line items with descriptions, quantities, unit prices
  • GST breakdown (subtotal, GST amount, total inc. GST)
  • Payment terms and bank details
Data Created

Invoices tab — invoice ID, sequential number, linked job, line items, subtotal, GST, total, status (Draft/Sent/Paid/Overdue), dates, and payment method.

Complexity Medium
Priority Week 1
Running Cost $0/mo

Tool 5

Expense Tracker

  4. Request logged in Reviews tab (date sent, customer, reviewed Y/N)

Google Sheets Data Model

One spreadsheet workbook, six tabs. This is the entire database. Emma sees exactly what's here — no hidden layers, no abstraction.

J Jobs

Core tab — every job Beau does gets a row here. This is the heartbeat of the system.

job_id date_created customer_name customer_phone customer_email address suburb service_type description scheduled_date scheduled_time status quote_id invoice_id notes photos_link

Status values: Enquiry → Scheduled → In Progress → Complete → Cancelled

C Customers

Auto-populated from Jobs. No manual customer entry needed — when a new customer name appears in a job, they get a row here automatically.

customer_id name phone email address suburb total_jobs total_spent last_job_date first_job_date review_status notes

Q Quotes

Every quote generated through the app. Links back to the job and forward to the invoice.

quote_id job_id customer_name date_created line_items subtotal gst total status sent_date accepted_date

Status values: Draft → Sent → Accepted / Declined

line_items: stored as a JSON string (e.g. description, qty, unit price per item) — the app reads and writes this, Emma sees the total

I Invoices

Every invoice issued. Emma's primary view for accounts receivable — filter by status to see what's outstanding.

invoice_id invoice_number job_id customer_name date_created line_items subtotal gst total status sent_date due_date paid_date payment_method

Status values: Draft → Sent → Paid / Overdue

Payment method: Cash / Bank Transfer / Card

E Expenses

Material purchases and business costs. Emma can filter by supplier to reconcile Reece and Walsh end-of-month statements.

expense_id date supplier description amount gst total job_id receipt_photo_url month

Supplier values: Reece / Walsh / Bunnings / Other

R Reviews

Tracks which customers have been asked for reviews and whether they've left one.

review_id job_id customer_name request_sent_date review_received platform review_date

Platform: Google / Facebook

review_received: Y / N

Why one spreadsheet?

Everything in one workbook means Emma can cross-reference instantly. Filter invoices by customer, check expenses against a job, see which months had the most revenue — all in the tool she already knows. No training, no new software.

Section 9

Risks and Mitigations

Every approach has risks. Here's what could go wrong and what we do about it.

Google changes Sheets API pricing

Low risk

Google Sheets API has been free for low-volume usage for over a decade. A solo plumber doing 20-30 jobs per month is well within any conceivable free tier. Even if pricing changed, the usage volume is negligible.

Mitigation: Data is in standard spreadsheet format. Can migrate to Supabase (free tier) or any database with minimal effort. No vendor lock-in.

Beau finds custom tools too basic

Medium risk

If Beau has used polished apps before and expects a certain level of refinement, the custom PWA might feel rough in comparison. The trade-off is simplicity and cost vs polish.

Mitigation: Iterative development. Features are added based on Beau's feedback. If at any point he'd prefer ServiceM8, the data in Google Sheets can be exported and imported. No sunk cost, no data loss.

Data loss

Low risk

What if someone accidentally deletes data from the spreadsheet? Or Google has an outage?

Mitigation: Google Sheets has built-in version history (every edit is saved, can revert any time). We'll also set up a simple automated backup script that copies the spreadsheet data daily. Belt and braces.

Emma prefers a "proper" accounting system

Medium risk

Emma or Tim's accountant might want proper accounting software for BAS lodgement, bank reconciliation, or reporting.

Mitigation: Xero Starter ($42/mo) integration is designed as a Phase 2 option. When the time comes, invoice data from Google Sheets maps directly to Xero via API or CSV import. The switch can happen at any time without disrupting the tools Beau uses on his phone.

Beau's phone isn't compatible

Low risk — validate phone model with Beau

PWAs work on any modern smartphone (iPhone or Android, from the last 5+ years). The only potential issue is very old devices with outdated browsers.

Mitigation: Confirm Beau's phone model. If it's a recent iPhone or Android device (2020+), it will work perfectly. PWAs have been supported on both platforms for years.

Understanding how work comes in today so we can capture and grow those channels.
