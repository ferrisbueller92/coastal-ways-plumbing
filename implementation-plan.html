Section 02.5 · New in v4.0

The Bigger Picture: Your AI Operating System

What v3.0 called "three friction layers" is actually the first three pieces of a bigger picture. You're getting a full AI Operating System — 8 canonical roles, 13 automated workflows, organised around 8 pillars covering every part of running a plumbing business.

What this means in plain English

Think of your AI OS as a small invisible team working alongside Beau, Emma and Tim. Each of the 8 "roles" is a job-shape (like "the person who chases quotes" or "the person who asks for reviews"). Behind each role is an AI agent that does the work, with one of you (Beau, Emma or Tim) reviewing and approving where it matters. The 13 workflows are the actual tasks these roles perform — from answering a missed call, through chasing a cold quote, to requesting a review after the invoice is paid.

The 8 pillars — every part of a plumbing business

P1 · Local Channel Presence

Website, Google Business Profile, directories (Yellow Pages, HiPages, TrueLocal), social media — being visible where locals already search.

P2 · Job Intake

Phone call, web form or emergency call → AroFlo job created. Emma's world — turning a ringing phone into a scheduled job.

P3 · Quote-to-Close

Site quote delivered → client decides → won or lost. The difference between ~40% and ~60% conversion is follow-up.

P4 · On-the-Job

Dispatch, ETA SMS, parts on van, photos, notes captured. Beau's world — keep the field work frictionless.

P5 · Invoice & Cash

Invoice generation, Stripe links, payment chase, Xero reconciliation. Strata on-invoice, cash on-spot.

P6 · Reputation & Referral

Review request after job → Google Business Profile, Trustpilot, Facebook. Word-of-mouth made repeatable.

P7 · Compliance & Licensing

Fair Trading NSW, ACMA SMS consent, insurance + ABN/TFN renewals, permit deadlines. Tim's world.

P8 · Signal & Review

Weekly / monthly dashboards — revenue trend, quote-to-close %, review count, cash-flow flag. Your Monday-morning numbers.

Same shape as "real" business departments

This 8-pillar view is the same structure we use for Marketing, Sales, Operations, Finance and Admin in much bigger businesses — just renamed for a plumbing business. Every workflow you run lines up with exactly one pillar. That's what makes this an operating system, not a scrapbook of scripts.

Section 03

AroFlo — What It Does For Beau

AroFlo is purpose-built for trades businesses. Here's every capability relevant to a solo plumber scaling to a small team.

J Job Management

Create jobs, assign status, track from enquiry to complete. Office can create jobs via email import or manual entry. Beau sees everything on the mobile app. Full job history for every customer.

Q Quoting

Generate quotes on-site from Beau's phone. Line items with markups, e-signature capture, email direct to customer. When a customer accepts, the quote auto-creates a job — no double handling.

$ Invoicing

Auto-generate invoices from completed jobs in about 30 seconds. Stripe payment links with "Pay Now" on PDF invoices. Payment status tracking. Payments sync to Xero.

C Scheduling

Drag-and-drop calendar. Beau sees tomorrow's jobs on his phone each evening. One-way Google Calendar sync available. Colour-coded by job type or priority.

I Inventory

Track what's on the van. Low stock alerts built in. Barcode scanning. Multi-location support (van, home storage, shed). As materials get used on jobs, stock counts update automatically.

P Purchase Orders

Create POs from Beau's phone. Direct ordering with Reece at live negotiated pricing. Auto invoice import from Reece. Manual PO creation for Walsh Plumbing Supplies and Bunnings.

R

Reece Integration Killer feature

Direct purchase orders with live negotiated pricing. PO instantly dispatched to Reece's system. Auto invoice import back into AroFlo. This is the single most valuable integration for a plumber — eliminates manual ordering, ensures correct pricing, and keeps paperwork clean.

M Mobile App

iOS and Android. Offline mode for no-signal sites. Photos, signatures, quotes, invoices, materials — all from the field. Beau never needs to sit at a computer.

X Xero Integration

Bi-directional but manually triggered. Pushes invoices, payments, and contacts to Xero. Pulls GL codes and bank feed payments back. Keeps accounting clean without auto-sync surprises.

Section 04

The First Three Workflows We Ship

Of the 13 workflows in the full AI OS, these three ship first because they unblock everything else. Each one maps directly into Beau's day-to-day: Layer 01 is how calls become jobs, Layer 02 is how Beau closes the day, Layer 03 is how the van never runs out of parts.

Why these three first

Layer 01 hydrates three workflows in the spine: Phone-to-AroFlo intake (M-W22), Form-to-AroFlo intake (M-W25) and optionally Voice-Receptionist (M-W26). Layer 02 closes out ETA / On-Job Customer Updates (M-W21) and Quote-to-Close (M-W61). Layer 03 IS a workflow — Stock Alerts & Reece Reorder (M-W64). Between them, these three Layers move 6 of the 13 workflows into "partial" or "built" state. The other 7 follow in Months 2–3.

Layer 01

Emma & Tim's Simplified Input

High priority Depends on API access

The problem

AroFlo's UI is complex. Emma and Tim have never used job management software. The learning curve could take weeks and cause frustration. If they give up, Beau is back to doing it all himself.

What we build: A clean, simple web form (or Telegram bot) where Emma types naturally and AroFlo gets populated via API or Zapier. She never opens AroFlo's full interface.

User Flow
  1. Emma gets a call from a property manager: "Blocked drain at 15 Beach Rd Terrigal, tenant available Thursday 2pm"
  2. Emma opens our simple form (or types in Telegram)
  3. Enters: customer name, address, job type (dropdown), preferred date/time, notes
  4. Our tool searches AroFlo for existing client (creates if new), creates the task, creates a schedule entry
  5. Beau sees the job appear on his phone in AroFlo
  6. Emma never opens AroFlo's full interface
Tech Zapier or AroFlo API
API calls per job 3–5
Within daily limit 2,000/day
Layer 02

Beau's End-of-Day Helper

Medium priority

The problem

Beau spends 5–10 minutes in the car after each job doing admin. Without office support, it'd be 20–30 minutes. We want to get it under 2 minutes.

What we build: A mobile-friendly tool that pulls today's jobs from AroFlo and prompts Beau to fill in what's missing. One screen, minimal tapping.

User Flow
  1. Beau finishes his last job, sits in the car
  2. Opens the helper tool on his phone
  3. Sees a list of today's jobs with status indicators — green = complete, amber = missing info
  4. For each incomplete job: prompted for materials used, time spent, notes, photos
  5. One tap submits to AroFlo
  6. If cash job: prompted for payment recording
  7. Summary: "5 jobs today. 3 invoiced. 2 need follow-up. Tomorrow: 4 jobs starting 7:30am."
Tech AroFlo API + PWA
Target time < 2 minutes
Notifications Telegram push
Layer 03

Inventory & Stock Alerts

Medium priority Verify AroFloGo inventory

The problem

Beau's #1 pain point. Showing up to a job without the right parts wastes time — a trip to Reece or Bunnings mid-job costs 30–60 minutes. Currently no system to track what's on the van.

What we build: A stock awareness layer using AroFlo's inventory module, enhanced with Telegram push notifications for low stock warnings.

How it Works
  1. Set up AroFlo inventory with Beau's common parts (washers, valves, connectors, fittings, etc.)
  2. Assign a "van" stock location in AroFlo
  3. As Beau uses materials on jobs (logged in AroFlo), van stock decreases automatically
  4. When items drop below threshold → alert via Telegram: "Low stock: 15mm copper connectors (2 left, reorder level: 5)"
  5. One tap creates a Reece purchase order in AroFlo
  6. Reece delivers to van pickup point or home

AroFlo native support

AroFlo's inventory module already has stock alert levels per business unit and per user. Low stock items automatically appear in the next stock order. We enhance this with instant Telegram push notifications so Beau knows the moment stock runs low, not just when he opens AroFlo.

Section 06

Communication Layer

60–70% of Beau's work is strata and real estate. Wrong keys, misaligned times, PMs not responding = wasted hours. AroFlo handles most of this natively.

What AroFlo already does

SMS Notifications

"Your plumber is on the way" — sent automatically when Beau sets job status to travel-to-site. Keeps tenants and PMs informed without Beau making calls.

Staff Arrival Alerts

Arrival time tracking for accountability with PMs. Proof of attendance for strata billing disputes.

Job Status Updates

Automated emails at each stage: booked, on-the-way, completed, invoiced. PMs get visibility without chasing Beau.

Custom Message Templates

Pre-written templates for common scenarios: booking confirmation, quote follow-up, payment reminder, job completion. One tap to send.

What we can add on top

Automated PM Status Updates

Via webhook: "Beau has arrived at 15 Beach Rd" → "Work completed, invoice attached". Property managers get real-time visibility without Beau needing to call or text manually.

Key Tracking

Custom field on AroFlo jobs: which keys are needed, where they're collected from, return status. Solves the "wrong keys" problem that wastes 30+ minutes per incident.

Pre-Job Tenant Confirmation

Automated SMS to tenant the day before: "Your plumber will arrive between 8–9am tomorrow. Please ensure access to [specific area]." Reduces no-access wasted trips.
