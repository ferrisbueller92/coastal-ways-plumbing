Section 01

Why v5.0 — The Pivot

v4.0 put AroFlo at the centre. Reality check today: AroFlo isn't being used because it's behind a paywall (~$250–400/mo plus up to $2,900 setup). For a pre-revenue plumbing business, that's the wrong order of operations.

The new principle

Tools follow revenue, not the other way around. We build the spine with free or near-free tools Beau and Emma already understand. When revenue justifies AroFlo (target: end of Sprint 8), we migrate. Until then, nothing blocks on a SaaS bill.

What changes from v4.0

  • AroFlo removed as Sprint 1–8 dependency
  • 8 of 13 workflows ship without it
  • 5 workflows wait for AroFlo migration in v6.0
  • Monthly cost drops from ~$300/mo to ~$45/mo
  • No $2,900 setup fee
  • Emma & Beau operate tools they already know

What stays the same

  • Same 8-week timeline, same exit date (Jun 18)
  • Same 3-person team (Beau, Emma, Tim)
  • Same 8 canonical AI roles
  • Website, domain, GBP, Search Console already live
  • Same success metrics
  • Dave's monthly check-in cadence post-Sprint 8

Section 03

The Lean Architecture

No single SaaS at the centre. Instead, a Google Sheet is the operational spine, with Xero handling money and Google Calendar handling time. Every human touchpoint runs through tools the team already owns.

Customers

Property managers, tenants, homeowners, strata

↓Inbound

↓Logged in

Contacts · Jobs · Quotes · Invoices · Reviews · Compliance

↓Runs through

Scheduling (shared)

Invoicing & payment

Emma's Phone

Customer SMS & socials

↓Signal back

GBP · Facebook · Instagram · Reviews

Why a Google Sheet at the centre?

It's free. Beau and Emma already know it. It syncs across phone + laptop. It exports cleanly when we migrate to AroFlo later. And for a business doing under ~30 jobs/month, a well-structured sheet is not a downgrade — it's the right tool.

Step 02

Reviews Loop (Manual)

~15 min setup · Emma owns forever
SMS template — save in Emma's Notes
Hi [Name], Beau here — thanks for having us out today. We're growing Coastal Ways Plumbing and Google reviews are huge for small businesses. Would you mind leaving a quick one? Takes 30 seconds: [review link]. Really appreciate it. — Beau
The rule
  • Every completed job → Emma sends the SMS within 2 hours. Not next day. Within the hour is best.
  • Tracker tab in CWP Master Sheet: Date · Customer · Phone · SMS Sent · Review Received · Stars · Response Needed.
  • Target: first 10 reviews in 30 days. Then the flywheel self-sustains.
  • Response SOP: every review answered in 24h. 5-star: thank + mention the service type (SEO). 3-star or lower: apologise + offer to fix + move offline.
Past-customer burst today

Beau pulls his last 10 completed jobs from memory/phone. Emma SMS-blasts all 10 today. Expect 2–3 reviews in 48 hours.

Section 10

The AroFlo Decision Gate — End of Sprint 8

At week 8 exit (Jun 18), we evaluate: is the business ready for AroFlo, or do we keep running lean?

Go to AroFlo if ALL of these are true

• Revenue is predictable and covers $400/mo + $2,900 setup within 3 months. • Job volume exceeds 30/month (manual Sheet starts to strain). • Beau is spending >30 min/day on admin. • A team member is being hired and onboarding friction matters.

Stay lean if ANY of these are true

• Revenue is still building, or seasonal. • Job volume is under 30/month. • The Sheet + Xero flow is working and Beau/Emma aren't complaining. • Cash is better deployed into marketing, a second van, or a tradie hire.

Either way, you're not locked in

Google Sheet exports to CSV. CSV imports into AroFlo. The lean stack is a springboard, not a dead end.
