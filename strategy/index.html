05 · The free levers most plumbers miss

The cheapest job to win is the one you already earned.

Beyond Google, three zero-cost levers quietly compound — a referred customer closes at a far higher rate than a cold Google lead, and a serviced hot-water system comes back every year. We systemise what Beau already does naturally.

🔁
LEVER 1 · REFERRALS

Turn one job into the next

A referred customer trusts you before you arrive and closes at the highest rate of any channel. We make the ask automatic: Beau mentions it at the job, a friendly SMS follows the review link, and every quote and invoice footer carries a "refer a friend" line. Zero cost, highest close rate.

🔧
LEVER 2 · REPEAT WORK

Bring them back every year

Hot water systems need annual servicing — a genuine, defensible reason to return. We log the install date and fire a reminder at 9–10 months, turning a one-off install into a recurring $150–300 service job. Same logic for bathroom-reno leak checks and drainage CCTV inspections.

📍
LEVER 3 · LOCAL COMMUNITY

Be the recommended name

94% of Australian households have an active Nextdoor neighbourhood, and the Coast's Facebook community groups run on "who do you recommend?" posts every week. We help you be the plumber people name — not the one advertising — across Nextdoor and five active local groups.

Why this matters more than any ad

A single recommendation in a Wyong Facebook group or Nextdoor reaches 50–200 local neighbours at the exact moment they need a plumber — with the trust of a neighbour's word, not a paid banner. It costs nothing but showing up well.

Two tiers — the second is the prize
Registered (50%+ owned) gets you listed and searchable. Certified (51%+ owned, plus $50K revenue or 6+ months trading) unlocks the big one: the Commonwealth Mandatory Set-Aside — agencies must approach you first, before any open tender, for contracts worth $80,000–$200,000. We map the path from one to the other.
The one gate to clear first
Supply Nation needs a Confirmation of Aboriginality (COA). Supply Nation accepts either a confirmation letter from a recognised Aboriginal organisation, or its own form — itself a statutory declaration — backed by two referees from a recognised Aboriginal organisation. That own-form route is usually the simpler path. Darkinjung LALC (02 4351 2930) is worth a call (community + local development pipeline), but not all Land Councils issue a COA letter — some only a Confirmation of Membership — so ask them specifically. One honest caveat: the Indigenous-business mark can't go on anything until registration is formally confirmed — so we prepare the space now and drop the badge the day it lands.

11 · What we're deliberately NOT doing

Focus is a strategy too.

Plenty of things look like marketing and quietly drain money at your scale. Here's what we're skipping on purpose — and why.

✕
Radio advertising

Emergency plumbing is driven by "I have a problem right now" search intent — not brand recall from an ad heard three weeks ago.

✕
hipages paid leads

$87–150+ per booked job, in a bidding war on someone else's platform — one operator we found spends $3,000/mo and wins about a third of what they quote. Your own Google ranking is a free asset you keep. (A free hipages profile for the listing is fine.)

✕
Oneflare / Yellow Pages premium

Oneflare is shutting down on 30 June 2026; Yellow Pages premium has declining reach for a single operator. The money works far harder on reviews and Google.

✕
Letterbox & car-window flyers

Cold letterbox drops get a 0.5–1.5% response. And leaflets under wipers on parked cars are actually illegal in NSW — a $250 on-the-spot fine under the littering laws. A branded service sticker left on the hot-water unit or meter box ("serviced by Coastal Ways · next check due · 0402 269 088") does the job instead — it stays with the customer for years and prompts the repeat call, with no litter risk.

✕
Chasing every partnership at once

We sequence the free, fast doors first (Council VendorPanel, Rheem/Rinnai) before the ones that need setup.

✕
Paying before the free groundwork works

Every paid step waits until the free foundation is proving itself. Cost follows results, not the other way around.
