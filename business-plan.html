02 — The Business

What Our Business Does

A plumbing company based in the Central Coast of NSW, specialising in residential, construction and emergency plumbing services. Services include plumbing repairs, installations, maintenance, drainage, gas and water solutions, blocked sewers, leak detection, new builds and commercial maintenance.

Target Market

  • Residential: Homeowners, property managers, tenants
  • Commercial: Small to medium businesses, commercial property owners, real estate developers
  • Emergency: Property owners requiring urgent plumbing repairs

Unique Selling Point

Personalised services, rapid response times, eco-friendly solutions, state of the art equipment (thermal imaging, water/gas leak detection, high pressure water jetting). This equipment and skill set is not commonly present in a regular plumbing company. Local presence in Jilliby and surrounding areas.

Business Goals

  • Year 1 — Leverage subcontracting (3 days/week) to provide foundation, grow direct business alongside.
  • Year 2 — Reduce subcontracting to minimum.
  • By 24 months — Direct work from own clientele only.
  • 5-year — Grow to comfortable financial level, decide on keeping small or hiring staff. May take on apprentices. Family manages finance/accounting.

History

Beau has been a plumber for over 10 years. 8 years at Bear Plumbing (high-end bathroom renovations, northern Sydney). 18 months at AMPS Plumbing (Central Coast maintenance, large customer base, 10 plumbers). Regular positive reviews from customers.

03 — Our Why, Vision & Mission

Our Why

To grow a business reflecting Beau's values: high-quality plumbing services with focus on customer satisfaction, reliability, cost-effectiveness. Strong desire to do plumbing the "right way" — care, consistency, respect.

07 — SWOT Analysis

Strengths

  • Skilled trade expertise — licensed, trained, problem-solving
  • Local market knowledge — codes, suppliers, customer needs
  • Personalised customer service — strong relationships
  • Fast response times — emergency and same-day
  • Quality workmanship — right first time, trust, referrals

Weaknesses

  • Limited brand recognition — new business
  • Smaller workforce — peak demand constraints
  • Cash flow sensitivity — high upfront costs
  • Dependence on owner — performance tied to Beau
  • Limited marketing reach — smaller budget
  • Seasonal fluctuations — demand drops

Opportunities

  • Growing demand — aging infrastructure, new housing
  • Emergency / 24/7 — premium pricing
  • Maintenance contracts — recurring revenue
  • Eco-friendly solutions — water saving, energy efficient
  • Online presence — Google, social media, reviews
  • Partnerships — builders, real estate, property managers
  • Service expansion — drain cleaning, gas fitting, hot water, renovations

Threats

  • Economic downturn — deferred work
  • High competition — established plumbers, franchises
  • Rising material costs — margin impact
  • Regulatory compliance — licensing, insurance, safety
  • Skilled labour shortages — hiring difficulty

Addressing Weaknesses

  • Brand recognition: Customer reviews, branded uniforms/vehicles/invoices, referral incentives
  • Smaller workforce: Clear scheduling, high-margin focus, subcontractors for peak, early apprentice
  • Cash flow: Deposits for large jobs, immediate invoicing, multiple payment options, supplier credit, cash reserve
  • Owner dependence: Job management software, delegate admin, train staff on comms
  • Marketing reach: Low-cost high-return, partner with RE agents, sponsor local events, before/after online
  • Seasonal: Preventative maintenance promotion, off-season discounts, annual budgeting

Addressing Threats

  • Competition: Differentiate on response time/professionalism/communication, clear upfront pricing, warranties/guarantees, niche specialisation
  • Price pressure: Educate on quality, tiered pricing, careful cost tracking, avoid under-pricing
  • Material costs: Lock supplier pricing, include price buffers, regular review, communicate validity periods
  • Regulatory: Keep licenses/insurance current, stay informed of code changes, safety checklists, compliance advisor
  • Economic downturns: Focus marketing on essential services, expand maintenance contracts, offer payment plans, control fixed costs
  • Labour shortages: Competitive pay, positive culture, trade school relationships
