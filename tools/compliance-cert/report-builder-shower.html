Overall severity
Minor = cosmetic / localised sealant · Moderate = active leak, limited spread · Significant = active leak with damage to structure or adjacent areas.
Consistent with a major defect (habitability / structural consequence)?
Waterproofing is a “major element” (s18E) but a leak is only a major defect if it also causes a habitability or structural consequence — which drives the 6-yr vs 2-yr warranty period. Not a legal determination.
Reference · what makes a waterproofing leak a “major defect”
Two-stage test under Home Building Act 1989 (NSW) s18E: (1) the defect must affect a major element — waterproofing qualifies; AND (2) it must cause, or be likely to cause, an inability to inhabit/use the building, destruction, or threat of structural collapse. NCAT case law (Vella v Mir; Ashton v Stevenson) confirms a waterproofing failure is not automatically a major defect without a proven consequence. Major defects carry a 6-year warranty; others 2 years.
