Why every element earns its place

A plain-English walkthrough of the two new report builders — Shower Leak Test and Sewer & Drain CCTV. The rule we built to: every field is here for a reason — it's required by a standard, demanded by a law, asked for in Beau's brief, or it protects you legally. If an element couldn't be justified, it was flagged for you to cut (see the amber box).

① The standards

Shower: AS 3740:2021 (wet-area waterproofing) under NCC 2022. Sewer: WSA 05-2020 v4.1 (the conduit condition-coding code) + AS/NZS 3500.2. These are the benchmarks a buyer, council or tribunal measures the report against.

② The law

Home Building Act 1989 (NSW) — waterproofing warranties (6 yr major / 2 yr other). Plumbing & Drainage Act 2011 (NSW) — licensing + what is/isn't a Certificate of Compliance. Regulator: Building Commission NSW; Central Coast Council for local drainage.

③ The liability

Once a report is handed to a buyer or agent, the Australian Consumer Law (s60) "due care & skill" guarantee and negligent-misstatement duty attach. Scope statements, disclaimers and a named licensed signature are how Beau manages that exposure.

⚑ Flagged for your call — couldn't fully justify, or deliberately softened

  • Sewer “Weather” — low value for an underground asset. Kept only because WSA records inspection conditions and Beau's template listed it. Cut it if it feels like clutter.
  • “Major defect?” / condition-rating fields are worded as professional flags, not legal rulings (“consistent with…”, “Uncertain”). That's deliberate: deciding a major defect or strata responsibility is NCAT's job — stating it as fact would create liability. Keep the soft wording.
  • Everything else validated against a standard, a law, Beau's brief, or a liability rationale. Nothing is in there "just because the template had it."
