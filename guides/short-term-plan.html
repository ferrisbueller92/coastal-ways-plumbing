2. Assumptions (challenge any of these)

These are the calls being made without a fresh interview. If any are wrong, the plan needs adjusting — flag back to Dave.

A1 — Beau is the only field person right now
No second plumber on the books. Job scheduling assumes 1 calendar lane, 1 vehicle, 1 capacity ceiling.
A2 — Emma works office hours from home, not in the field
Phone-based, laptop or phone for Sheet/Gmail/Outlook. Not on-site. SMS sent from her personal mobile until/unless we add a business SMS number later.
A3 — Tim's involvement is light-touch advisory + compliance oversight
Not daily ops. He owns the Compliance tab in the Master Sheet (licence renewals, insurance dates) and provides 40+ years of business sanity-check. He does not need Workspace email, just Sheet view-access.
A4 — Beau prefers voice + photos over typing
All field capture leans toward voice notes (Telegram bot in Sprint 3) and photo-trigger workflows. Don't ask Beau to fill in forms while on a job.
A5 — Emma is comfortable with Google Sheets and Gmail
Not a Sheets power user, but can copy-paste, fill rows, follow templates. No formulas she has to author. Templates we build do the heavy lifting.
A6 — Customer payment is mostly card or bank transfer
Cash-on-spot is real for emergency public callouts (~30–40% of work) but most strata/PM jobs get invoiced and paid by EFT. Stripe link covers the card path. No need for EFTPOS hardware yet.
A7 — Quote turnaround target is 24 hours
Beau gives Emma the scope (voice + photos + 1-line context). Emma drafts in the Quote template within the same day. Beau approves. Quote out same-day or next-morning.

  • All 8 directory listings submitted

  • Beau is taking 3 photos per job without prompting

  • Tim has flagged at least one upcoming compliance date proactively

If we miss these gates
Sprint 3 (Field Capture / Telegram bot) shifts back a week. We hold Sprint 2 until the manual flow is bulletproof — automation amplifies whatever's already working, so it must be working manually first.
