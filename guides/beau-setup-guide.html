Getting Online

Your step-by-step guide to setting up Coastal Ways Plumbing's digital presence.

For Beau Petterson • Prepared by Dave Calder • Last updated: 4 April 2026

We're setting up the digital foundation for Coastal Ways Plumbing. Think of it like connecting the plumbing in a new house — the pipes (domain, Google, email) need to be in place before the fixtures (website, reviews, booking) work properly.

1

Create a Google account for the business

One login for everything Google — Maps, Reviews, Calendar
Do this first
Why: Dave set up the Google Business Profile using his personal account. You need your own Google account so you own it. This account controls your Google Maps listing, Google Reviews, and everything Google-related for the business.
  1. Go to accounts.google.com/signup
  2. Tap Create account → choose "For work or my business"
  3. Fill in:
    FieldWhat to type
    First nameCoastal Ways
    Last namePlumbing
    Emailcoastalwaysplumbing@gmail.com
    PasswordSomething strong — write it down
  4. Add your phone (0402 269 088) for recovery
  5. Complete the signup

When you're done

Text Dave the Gmail address. He'll transfer the Google Business Profile ownership to your account so you control it.

2

Send assets to Dave

The files needed to finish the website
Whenever ready
Why: The website has placeholder spots for your photos. Real photos of you and your work build trust faster than any words on a page. A property manager deciding between two plumbers will pick the one who looks real.
  • Logo files from Monique — the actual image files (SVG or PNG), not the PDF. If you're not sure what you have, send everything and Dave will sort it out.
  • 3-5 job photos — before/after shots, you working, your van, your equipment.
  • Loos font files — ask Monique: "Do you have the Loos font files in web format?" If she doesn't know what that means, just ask for "the font files."
Don't have photos ready? No stress. Take a few next time you're on a job: one "before" showing the problem, one "during" of you working, one "after" of the finished result. Send them to Dave whenever.
3

Facebook Business Page

A separate page for the business (not your personal Facebook)
Why: Customers check Facebook to see if a business is real. A page with your logo, some job photos, and a few reviews tells them you're legit. It's also free advertising when people share or recommend you.
  1. Open Facebook on your phone
  2. Tap the menu (three lines) → Pages → Create
  3. Fill in:
    FieldWhat to enter
    Page nameCoastal Ways Plumbing
    CategoryPlumber
    BioLicensed plumber on the Central Coast. Residential, commercial & emergency. Call 0402 269 088.
  4. Upload the Coastal Ways logo as the profile picture
  5. Upload a cover photo (a job photo or the van)
  6. Add your phone number, website (coastalwaysplumbing.com), and service area in the Page Info section

When you're done

Share the page link with Dave. He'll link it from the website.

4

Instagram Business Account

Before/after job photos that show your quality work
Why: Instagram is where younger homeowners and property managers look. Before/after photos of your work are the most powerful trust signal you can have — zero cost, just snap photos you're already taking.
  1. Open Instagram → if you don't have one, create an account:
    FieldWhat to enter
    Usernamecoastalwaysplumbing
    Profile nameCoastal Ways Plumbing | Central Coast Plumber
    BioLicensed plumber. 10+ yrs experience. Residential, Commercial, Emergency 24/7. Central Coast NSW. 0402 269 088

6

AroFlo setup

Your job management system — quoting, invoicing, scheduling
Next session with Dave
Why: AroFlo is where your jobs, quotes, invoices, and schedule live. It connects to Xero for accounting and has a direct link to Reece for ordering parts. Dave is building tools on top of AroFlo to make it faster for you in the field and easier for Emma and Tim in the office.
  1. Go to aroflo.com and sign up for the AroFloGo 14-day free trial
  2. During the trial, Dave needs to check a few things:
    • Does the Go plan include API access? (needed for automation)
    • Does it include the Reece supplier integration?
    • Does it include Zapier? (connects AroFlo to other tools)
  3. Dave will help configure job types, quote templates, and invoice templates

Cost

AroFloGo is $39/month after the trial. This replaces needing separate tools for quoting, invoicing, scheduling, and job tracking.

7

Start asking for Google Reviews

The single most important thing for getting found online
After first few jobs
Why: Google Reviews are the #1 factor in local search ranking. Every 5-star review pushes you higher in "plumber near me" results. Competitors with 50 reviews will always rank above someone with none — even if your website is better. Start from your very first job.
  1. After completing a job where the customer is happy, simply ask: "If you were happy with the work, I'd really appreciate a Google review — it helps people find us."
  2. You can text them a direct link to your review page (Dave will give you this link once GBP is verified under your account)
  3. Aim for 1-2 reviews per week. By month 3, you'll have 10-20 reviews — more than most new plumbers on the Coast
Tip: The best time to ask is right after you've shown them the finished work and they've said something positive. Don't be shy — customers want to help, they just need a nudge.
