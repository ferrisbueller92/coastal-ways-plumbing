Getting Online

Your step-by-step guide to setting up Coastal Ways Plumbing's digital presence.

For Beau Petterson • Prepared by Dave Calder • 4 April 2026

We're setting up the digital foundation for Coastal Ways Plumbing. Think of it like connecting the plumbing in a new house — the pipes (domain, Google, email) need to be in place before the fixtures (website, reviews, booking) work properly.

Most of these are one-time tasks that take 5-10 minutes each. Once done, they work forever.

1

Create a Google account for the business

One login for everything Google — Maps, Reviews, Calendar
Why: When someone searches "plumber near me" on their phone, Google shows a map with local businesses first. Without a Google account, Coastal Ways is invisible on that map. This is the #1 way people find local plumbers.
  1. Go to accounts.google.com/signup
  2. Tap Create account → choose "For work or my business"
  3. Fill in:
    FieldWhat to type
    First nameCoastal Ways
    Last namePlumbing
    Emailcoastalwaysplumbing@gmail.com
    PasswordSomething strong — write it down
  4. Add your phone (0402 269 088) for recovery
  5. Complete the signup

When you're done

Write down the email and password somewhere safe. Send Dave the email address (not the password). You'll use this login for the next step.

2

Set up Google Business Profile

Your listing on Google Maps — how new customers find you
Why: This is the single most important thing you can do to get phone calls from new customers. When someone in Wyong searches "plumber near me," Google shows a map with businesses ranked by reviews and relevance. This puts you on that map. It's free.
  1. Go to business.google.com (easier on a laptop)
  2. Sign in with the Google account you just created
  3. Tap "Add your business to Google"
  4. Search for "Coastal Ways Plumbing" — tap "Add your business"
  5. Fill in:
    FieldWhat to enter
    Business nameCoastal Ways Plumbing
    CategoryStart typing Plumber and select it
    Location customers can visit?Select No (you go to them)
    Service areaType Central Coast and select NSW
    Phone0402 269 088
    Websitecoastalwaysplumbing.com
  6. Verify your business — pick phone verification if offered (fastest). Google calls or texts a code to your number. Type it in. Done.
  7. Once verified, add:
    • Hours: Mon-Fri 7am-5pm, Sat-Sun "Emergency calls only"
    • Description: "Licensed plumber serving the Central Coast. 10+ years experience in residential, commercial and emergency plumbing. Leak detection, blocked drains, hot water systems, gas fitting, bathroom renovations. Available 24/7 for emergencies."
    • Photos: Upload your logo + 3-5 job photos

When you're done

Your listing starts appearing on Google Maps within 24-48 hours. Every Google Review you get from a customer makes you rank higher. Ask happy customers to leave a review — it's the best free marketing you can do.

3

Send assets to Dave

The files Dave needs to finish the website
Why: Real photos of you and your work build trust faster than any words on a page. A property manager deciding between two plumbers will pick the one who looks real. Stock photos scream "I just started yesterday."
  • Logo files from Monique — the actual image files (SVG or PNG), not the PDF. If you're not sure what you have, send everything and Dave will sort it out.
  • 3-5 job photos — before/after shots, you working, your van, your equipment.
  • Loos font files — ask Monique: "Do you have the Loos font files in web format?" If she doesn't know what that means, just ask for "the font files."
Don't have photos ready? No stress. Take a few next time you're on a job: one "before" showing the problem, one "during" of you working, one "after" of the finished result. Send them to Dave whenever.
4

beau@coastalwaysplumbing.com instead of an Outlook address
Why: When a property manager gets a quote from beau@coastalwaysplumbing.com, it looks established and professional. enquirebpplumbing@outlook.com looks temporary. This is free to set up.

When to do this

Dave will let you know when the domain is ready and walk you through the setup. Takes about 10 minutes.

Dave will guide you
5

Facebook Business Page

A separate page for the business (not your personal Facebook)
Why: Customers check Facebook to see if a business is real. A page with your logo, some job photos, and a few reviews tells them you're legit. It's also free advertising when people share or recommend you.
  1. Open Facebook on your phone
  2. Tap the menu (three lines) → Pages → Create
  3. Fill in:
    FieldWhat to enter
    Page nameCoastal Ways Plumbing
    CategoryPlumber
    BioLicensed plumber on the Central Coast. Residential, commercial & emergency. Call 0402 269 088.
  4. Upload the Coastal Ways logo as the profile picture
  5. Upload a cover photo (a job photo or the van)
  6. Add your phone number, website, and service area in the Page Info section

When you're done

Share the page link with Dave. He'll link it from the website.

6

Instagram Business Account

Before/after job photos that show your quality work
Why: Instagram is where younger homeowners and property managers look. Before/after photos of your work are the most powerful trust signal you can have — zero cost, just snap photos you're already taking.
  1. Open Instagram → if you don't have one, create an account:
    FieldWhat to enter
    Usernamecoastalwaysplumbing
    Profile nameCoastal Ways Plumbing | Central Coast Plumber
    BioLicensed plumber. 10+ yrs experience. Residential, Commercial, Emergency 24/7. Central Coast NSW. 0402 269 088
  2. Tap your profile → Settings → Account → Switch to Professional Account → choose Business
  3. Upload the logo as your profile picture
  4. Add coastalwaysplumbing.com as your website link
Don't stress about posting yet. Just get it set up. When you start taking before/after photos on jobs, that becomes your content. One photo pair per week is plenty.
