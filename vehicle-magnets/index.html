The one question that decides it

What colour are the vehicles you're putting these on? That's really the only thing that changes the answer:

  • White, silver or light-coloured → Concept A. Highest contrast, punches hardest.
  • Dark (navy, charcoal, black) → Concept B. The light sign stands out on dark paint.
  • A mix of colours → Concept C. Holds up on anything.

Let us know the vehicle colours and which orientation suits (landscape sits neatly on ute and van doors; portrait suits taller van panels), and we'll finalise your pick.

A few notes for the sign printer

  • Ask for a printed proof before the full run. Big flat colour areas are where cheap magnet printing can show banding — a single proof confirms the colour before you commit.
  • These are RGB files. Ask the printer to convert to CMYK and check the teal holds — it's the one colour that can shift.
  • A plain rounded-rectangle cut is all that's needed — the wave on Concept B is printed, not a cut shape.
  • Crispest possible logo: if mpstudioco still has your original logo file (an .ai, .svg or .eps), send it through and we'll swap it in — it'll be razor-sharp at any size. What's on here already prints cleanly at this scale.

What we left off, on purpose

  • Email address. No one emails a ute driving past, and it would shrink the phone number. Worth landing on one address for everything else, though — the website currently shows one and Beau's email signature another.
  • QR code. No one scans a moving car — it would just be clutter.
  • The name typed out again. Your logo already says "coastal ways / PLUMBING", so repeating it in text beside the logo would just look doubled-up.
